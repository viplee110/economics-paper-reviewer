# Empirical Causal Paper Review Lens

Use for reduced-form empirical, causal inference, policy evaluation, and applied micro/macro papers.

## Must Check

- What is the causal question?
- What is the identification strategy?
- What variation identifies the effect?
- What assumptions are required?
- Are threats to identification addressed?
- Are data, sample construction, and measurement credible?
- Are results economically meaningful, not just statistically significant?
- Are robustness checks relevant rather than decorative?
- Is external validity discussed honestly?

## Fatal Risks

- identification strategy does not answer the stated question
- omitted variable, selection, timing, or anticipation threat is central and unresolved
- measurement invalidates the main claim
- results are not robust to obvious alternatives
- data or sample construction is opaque
- causal language exceeds the design

## Fixable Risks

- incomplete robustness map
- unclear institutional background
- weak effect-size interpretation
- missing pre-trend or balance evidence where relevant
- unclear sample restrictions
