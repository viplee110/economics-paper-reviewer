# Theory Paper Review Lens

Use for economic theory, applied theory, mechanism design, information design, game theory, contract theory, market design, search, networks, IO theory, and related theory papers.

## Must Check

- What is the central economic question?
- What is the primitive or modeling insight?
- What is the main theorem sentence?
- Why can existing theory not obtain the result?
- Is the theorem bite strong enough?
- Are assumptions minimal, interpretable, and not defensive?
- Is the proof route credible?
- Are boundary cases and counterexamples considered?
- Does the model begin from a visible economic force rather than formal machinery?

## Fatal Risks

- no central theorem
- old theory absorbs the result
- weak theorem bite
- artificial assumptions
- model complexity hiding a weak idea
- proof only works in a special case not acknowledged
- contribution sentence becomes longer instead of sharper

## Fixable Risks

- weak theorem exposition
- missing economic interpretation of assumptions
- unclear proof roadmap
- insufficient comparison to nearby models
- architecture too theorem-package-like
