# Experimental / Behavioral Review Lens

Use for laboratory experiments, field experiments, survey experiments, behavioral economics, and mechanism experiments.

## Must Check

- What behavioral or causal mechanism is tested?
- Is treatment assignment credible?
- Are instructions, incentives, and timing aligned with the hypothesis?
- Is sample size and power adequate?
- Are outcomes measured cleanly?
- Are multiple testing and heterogeneity handled responsibly?
- Is external validity framed honestly?
- Does the experiment distinguish the proposed mechanism from alternatives?

## Fatal Risks

- design cannot identify the claimed mechanism
- treatment confounds multiple channels
- incentives or instructions undermine interpretation
- sample size is too small for the claimed result
- attrition, noncompliance, or balance issues are central and unresolved
- interpretation exceeds the design

## Fixable Risks

- unclear experimental protocol
- missing balance or attrition table
- insufficient mechanism checks
- weak discussion of external validity
- overbroad behavioral interpretation
