# Structural / IO Empirical Review Lens

Use for structural estimation, empirical IO, dynamic models, demand estimation, auctions, market design empirics, counterfactual policy simulations, and model-based empirical papers.

## Must Check

- What economic object is estimated?
- Why is a structural model needed?
- How does the model map to the data?
- Which moments or likelihood features identify key parameters?
- Are counterfactuals credible under the estimated model?
- Are assumptions testable, interpretable, or at least transparent?
- Is there reduced-form evidence supporting the core mechanism?
- Are estimation details and computational reproducibility adequate?

## Fatal Risks

- model-data link is unclear
- key parameters are weakly identified
- counterfactuals rely on untested model features
- estimates are driven by functional-form assumptions
- no reduced-form or descriptive evidence supports the mechanism
- code or algorithm details are insufficient for reproducibility

## Fixable Risks

- unclear moment selection
- insufficient sensitivity analysis
- weak exposition of identification
- missing computational diagnostics
- counterfactual interpretation too broad
