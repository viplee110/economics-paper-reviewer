# Econometrics / Methodology Review Lens

Use for econometric theory, statistical methodology, identification theory, inference, machine learning for economics, and methodological papers.

## Must Check

- What problem does the method solve?
- What assumptions are required?
- Are assumptions weaker, clearer, or more useful than alternatives?
- Are theorems stated with correct scope?
- Are proofs credible and complete at the reviewed level?
- Are simulations or examples aligned with the theorem?
- Does the method improve applied practice or theoretical understanding?
- Are limitations and failure modes clear?

## Fatal Risks

- theorem does not imply the claimed method property
- assumptions are stronger than advertised
- proof sketch hides a central gap
- simulations do not test the relevant failure modes
- method is dominated by existing approaches without explanation
- practical implementation is underspecified

## Fixable Risks

- unclear notation
- weak comparison to benchmark methods
- missing finite-sample or boundary examples
- vague implementation guidance
- theorem exposition too compressed
