# Survey / Literature Review Paper Review Lens

Use this lens for survey papers, literature reviews, handbook-style chapters, agenda-setting essays, and Journal of Economic Literature style papers.

## Central Question

Ask what intellectual map the paper gives the reader:

- Which literature, debate, or research field does the paper organize?
- What is the organizing framework?
- Does the paper synthesize mechanisms and evidence, or merely list papers?
- What would a specialist learn that is not obvious from reading abstracts?
- What would an adjacent-field reader understand after reading it?

## Must Check

- The boundary of included and excluded literature is explicit.
- The paper has a taxonomy, framework, or conceptual map rather than only chronology.
- Major strands of the literature are represented fairly.
- Competing mechanisms, interpretations, or empirical findings are distinguished.
- Evidence strength is graded rather than treated as uniform.
- Key claims are grounded in cited papers and marked provisional if not verified.
- The paper explains unresolved questions and future research opportunities.
- The manuscript adds value relative to existing surveys.

## Fatal Risks

- Bibliography without synthesis.
- No organizing framework.
- Selective or biased literature coverage.
- Central claims unsupported by verified cited evidence.
- Major papers, methods, or debates are mischaracterized.
- Consensus, policy implications, or research closure are overstated.
- The contribution is unclear relative to existing surveys or handbook chapters.

## Fixable Risks

- Missing taxonomy.
- Unclear field boundary.
- Too much chronological listing.
- Weak evidence grading.
- Insufficient comparison across mechanisms or methods.
- Limited discussion of unresolved tensions.
- Weak reader roadmap.
- Too little explanation of why this survey is timely.

## Review Guidance

Do not demand a new theorem, causal estimate, structural estimation, or original experiment as the central contribution. A strong survey contribution can be a clearer map, a sharper taxonomy, a correction of field narratives, a synthesis of scattered mechanisms, or a research agenda that changes what readers see as open.

At the same time, do not excuse weak scholarship because the paper is a review. The paper still needs verified claims, balanced coverage, useful organization, and an explicit contribution.
