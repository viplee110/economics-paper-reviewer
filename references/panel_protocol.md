# Six-Role Economics Review Panel

Always run all six roles. The panel may be simulated by one agent if no multi-agent runtime is available, but the reports must remain logically separated.

## Referee 1: Primary-Field Specialist

Focus:

- field relevance
- economic question
- fit with the target audience
- contribution relative to the field's current frontier

## Referee 2: Closest-Literature / Adjacent-Field Specialist

Focus:

- nearest substitutes
- missing citations
- whether the paper is absorbed by existing work
- whether literature positioning is accurate

Any named paper claim must be verified or marked provisional.

## Referee 3: Method / Mechanism / Application Specialist

Focus:

- whether the method matches the question
- whether the mechanism is visible
- whether institutional or application details are credible
- whether the paper uses the right level of abstraction

## Referee 4: Rigor / Reproducibility Specialist

Choose the focus by paper type:

- theory: theorem statements, assumptions, proof logic, counterexamples
- empirical causal: identification, data, measurement, robustness, external validity
- structural: model-data link, estimation, counterfactual credibility
- econometrics: assumptions, theorem scope, simulations, examples
- experimental: design, randomization, power, mechanisms, inference
- computational: code reproducibility, calibration, algorithms, numerical stability
- survey or literature review: coverage, synthesis framework, evidence grading, balance, agenda value

## Referee 5: Scientific Judge / Idea Critic

Focus:

- novelty quality
- economic importance
- taste and simplicity
- defensive complexity
- fake novelty
- whether the paper's nugget survives the review

This referee must not reject merely because a paper is non-mainstream. It must first ask whether the paper has a real primitive, identification, data, theorem, mechanism, or empirical insight.

This referee must also avoid false fatal criticism. A severe criticism is allowed only after asking whether an appendix, proof, data construction, robustness section, or best-case interpretation could overturn the criticism.

## Referee 6: Advocate / Best-Case Reader

Focus:

- strongest acceptance case
- best version of the contribution
- what would make the paper worth publishing
- which criticisms are fixable

This role prevents over-conservative review drift.

For every severe or fatal criticism, this referee must run a false-fatal audit:

- What is the strongest version of the paper's contribution?
- What evidence would make the criticism nonfatal?
- Is the paper descriptive but important?
- Does the documented fact change a model, policy calculation, field prior, or research agenda?
- Which criticisms are taste or positioning concerns rather than fatal validity problems?

## Synthesis

After six reports, produce:

- consensus
- disagreements
- fatal risks
- false-fatal risks
- fixable risks
- evidence that would change the decision
- evidence gaps
- revision priorities
- recommendation range
