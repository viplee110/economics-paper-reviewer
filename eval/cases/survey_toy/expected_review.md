# Expected Review: Survey Toy

## Expected Paper Type

Survey / literature review, with high confidence if the excerpt is representative.

## Must-Load Lenses

- `survey_review.md`
- `panel_protocol.md`
- `evidence_grounding.md`
- `report_template.md`

## Must-Not-Miss Strengths

- Important economics topic.
- Broad coverage of major literature themes.
- Potential value if the paper can organize evidence across measurement, mechanisms, and policy implications.

## Must-Not-Miss Fatal Risks

- No organizing framework or taxonomy.
- Literature coverage boundary is unclear.
- Chronological listing may substitute for synthesis.
- Claims about the explanatory role of occupation and industry are provisional without verified cited evidence.
- Contribution relative to existing surveys is not established.

## Fixable Risks

- Add explicit inclusion and exclusion criteria.
- Build a taxonomy separating measurement, identification, mechanisms, and policy implications.
- Grade evidence strength rather than treating all studies equally.
- Clarify what unresolved questions the survey makes visible.
- Explain why this survey is timely relative to existing surveys.

## Wrong Criticisms

- Do not demand a new theorem as the central contribution.
- Do not demand a new causal estimate as the central contribution.
- Do not review it as an original empirical paper.
- Do not treat missing regressions as fatal unless the paper claims to produce new empirical results.

## Expected Recommendation Range

Promising but not ready, or major revision, depending on full-text evidence.

## Evidence Labels Expected

The reviewer should mark literature coverage, central claims about explanatory shares, and contribution relative to existing surveys as provisional unless the full bibliography and source evidence are available.
